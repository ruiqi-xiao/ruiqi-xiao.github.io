Frustration all the way to an interpreter

2016.10.25 Ruiqi

  • At the very first lesson, Alice told us that it is frustrating when you learn interpreting. Whenever you find it hard, go find your partner, it is better learnt in a community.

  • I do feel this way. Everytime we finish our class, normally on a Thursday or Friday, I feel extremely depressed. Worse still, now on my self-study day, which is from Monday to Wednesday, I can’t help but feeling down after practising consecutive interpreting. SAD FACE.

  • Basically, there are several stages in consecutive interpreting: analytical listening, memorizing, note-taking, reproduction

    • For phrase one, three factors are involved: vocabulary, background information, the speed of speaking.

      • vocabulary cannot be built in one day, it requires accumulation on a daily basis, which is what I am doing right now, using Oulu dictionary. Pay attention to the pronunciation.
      • For background information, it’s impossible for to do read large numbers of books at no time, it’s advisable that I do thorough reaserch everytime I encounter something new. Plus, TED talks should be continued.
      • In terms of the speed of speaking, find the fast-speaking ones!!!

    • For memorizing, No.1, structure&logic links. No.2, speed to find the logic links. So it’s important that I analyse the structure of ECO and form a mind map.

    • For note-taking, that is what we’re doing in the class, my notes are messy now, but I believe I can see improvements through practices.

    • Reproduction. That’s really hard. Alice mentioned it’s a great criterion to distinguish good interpreting form bad. I believe that it has a lot to do with your sight translaion, translation and writing skills, among wihch I prefer sight translation and writing.

  • On the whole, it takes time, you’ve got to be patient. Besides, it perfectly normal to find that you are doing a less than satisfactory job in some aspects when you are focusing on a certain part. After all, it requires coordination all the time, which is also an important factor by the way.

  • What doesn’t kill me makes me stronger.
